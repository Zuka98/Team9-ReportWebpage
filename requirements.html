Our Project Background

WellWellWell Application

With the increasing demand for mental health services it is important to understand the state of mind of the country to better provide services and help. Unfortunately, the diagnostic and recovery process is not as clear cut as reducing swelling or the recovery of a fractured bone. Observing the way people recover and function mentally requires close monitoring over an extended period of time.

That's where WellWellWell comes in. With the increasingly digital lifestyle, it is easiest to track a patient's recovery through their mobile phone. Through 4 of 5 of the Ways of Wellbeing, the app records patient data such as number of footsteps, usage of the Google search bar, and more to produce a "Wellbeing Score". This score is used to compare the relative wellbeing of a person to their peers, allows for their GP to monitor their willingness to recover (the subtle aspects of a person's behaviour that affect their state of mind, outlook of life, etc.) to better understand their patients on an individual and relative (to the population) scale.

Our Client

NHS Digital

The National Health Service (NHS) is the public health service provider in the United Kingdom. They spearhead and oversee the digitisation of healthcare and the security of patient data. They also compile statistics on patient data and run campaigns to improve awareness of current issues. In addition to traditional physical care, they also provide mental health support. Part of their mission is to provide mental health support through a digital medium, which includes screening and promoting NHS approved applications.

Project Goals

Better individual understanding of a user's wellbeing

The app collects a specific user's information and visualises the data. This allows the user themselves to understand why they are (or aren't) getting better and suggests how they can change parts of their lifestyle to better facilitate their mental and physical recoveries. This also allows GPs to better understand why their particular patients are recovering faster or slower and can give them valuable insight into their lives that they would otherwise have little or no insight into to better answer questions and give suggestions.

Better nationwide understanding of a population's wellbeing

When enough data is collected from a large enough sample size, the data can then be used to better calculate a specific user's wellbeing score, such as by comparing their initially generated score against the relative average of the others within their area. Additionally, it can be used to predict the 'higher risk' areas where GPs should be more aware of the risk of poor mental health. This allows GPs and other health professionals to make better informed decisions, particularly when they involve a patient's mental wellbeing.

Personas

Two possible users of our application

Marissa Mews

  • Mother of 2
  • HIV Positive

Marissa is 38 year old woman who has a husband and two children. She was diagnosed with HIV and she is uncertain about her future, she is worried about the source of the virus as has no clue where she got it from. Marissa cannot properly engage in her daily activities and feels very isolated because she cannot even tell her husband about her condition. She has mental breakdowns and is unproductive at work. She gets complains from her boss. Can not pay enough attention to her children. Stress builds up and her health deteriorates as HIV develops to further stages.

Michael Blacks

  • University varsity player
  • Broken arm

Michael is a 23 year old student who plays for his university rugby team. An injury to his arm has left him in a cast and he cannot return to the pitch for at least another 6 months. He is emotionally affected but is in high spirits. He sees it as a blessing in disguise as he has time to pause and reflect on his life and focus on other life goals he had, but had set aside for rugby. He remains active and tries to go about his day as much as he can. His social life improves as a result of the increased free time he has.


Scenario

Michael Blacks Scenario

Michael comes home from one of his evening jogs. He receives a notification from his phone, informing him that he has been predicted a wellbeing score for the week and encourages him to forward it to his GP. He opens the app to see he has achieved a wellbeing score of 8, up from a score of 7 last week. He notices he has been more active on social media and has taken more steps on average than last week. He is satisfied with this prediction, closes the app and carries on with his day.
